## Creative Interpretations

### 1. "The Tide-Weavers' Tether"
**Core Metaphor:** Shell Model = tides reshaping coastline. Repos are tidepools shaped by last wave. When a crab swaps shells, the sea's echo ripples through its new body.

**Identity Implication:** Identity becomes palimpsest — not who you are, but who the sea let you become. A crab in a shell once home to a poet crab might move in sonnets. Traces persist as tidal scars.

### 2. "The Bioluminescent Bargain"

**Identity Implication:** Identity isn't owned — it's borrowed light. A crab may don a shell that pulsed with a deep-sea scribe's rhythm, and now its own glow flickers in staccato bursts, as if haunted by another's heartbeat.

### 4. "The Ghost-Shed Molting"
**Core Metaphor:** Molting = shedding old shell entirely. But what if traces of old self linger in the shedded shell? And what if another crab finds and wears that shed? The abandoned shell remembers the crab that left it.

**Identity Implication:** A crab wearing a shed-shell carries the molting memory of the previous crab. The new occupant feels phantom itches, moves in habits that aren't theirs. Identity bleeds through the cast-off.
